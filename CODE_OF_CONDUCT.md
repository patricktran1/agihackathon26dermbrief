# Code of Conduct

DermBrief welcomes rigorous, respectful collaboration across medicine, research, design, and engineering.

## Expected behavior

Contributors should:

- critique ideas and implementations without attacking people
- state uncertainty and disclose relevant limitations
- treat clinical safety concerns as substantive review input
- protect private, confidential, and health-related information
- give credit for meaningful contributions
- remain open to correction when evidence changes

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, deliberate humiliation, doxxing, sexualized conduct, retaliation, and knowingly misleading clinical or technical claims are not acceptable.

## Enforcement

Repository maintainers may edit or remove comments, reject contributions, limit participation, or ban contributors when conduct threatens the project or its community. Reports should be made privately to the maintainer rather than through a public issue.

Good-faith disagreement is welcome. Bad-faith degradation of people, evidence, or safety boundaries is not.
